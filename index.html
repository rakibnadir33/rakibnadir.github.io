About Me

I’m Rakib Mahmud Nadir, an aspiring offensive security engineer with over two years of focused, self-driven learning in penetration testing, vulnerability assessment, and ethical hacking. I’ve built my foundations through hands-on practice rather than theory alone, spending most of my time designing and attacking realistic lab environments, Active Directory test networks, and CTF-style challenge scenarios. For me, the learning process doesn’t end at exploitation. I prioritize understanding root causes, documenting attack paths, and mapping every finding back to practical remediation and defensive hardening.

My core interests lie in red team tradecraft, adversary-style attack simulation, and the intersection between offensive security techniques and real-world defensive controls. I am especially focused on Active Directory security, privilege escalation paths, Kerberos-based attacks, and common configuration weaknesses that lead to enterprise compromise. Alongside this, I actively study web application security aligned with the OWASP Top 10, where I enjoy converting theoretical vulnerabilities into reproducible lab exercises, walkthroughs, and small research-driven projects.

Offensive Security Methodology

I follow a structured approach to attacking lab environments that mirrors how real adversaries operate while staying ethical and controlled.

01

Recon & Enumeration

Map attack surface with Nmap, directory brute-forcing, and service fingerprinting to identify exposed assets, misconfigurations, and weak entry points.

02

Initial Access

Exploit web and network vulnerabilities, craft Metasploit payloads, and leverage weak credentials to obtain controlled initial footholds.

03

Privilege Escalation

Perform local enumeration, abuse misconfigurations, and execute AD-style attacks to move from low-privilege to higher-impact access in the environment.

04

Post-Exploitation & Reporting

Demonstrate impact in a controlled way, maintain access where appropriate, and convert technical findings into clear, actionable reports for teams to remediate.
